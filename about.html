Ninkii seexdaa sicii dibi dhal.

A sleeping herdsmen's cow births a bull calf.
This goes back to a story about two herders whose cows simultaneously gave birth to two calves. One gives birth to a bull calf, the other to a heifer calf. While the cow's owner, which had calved a heifer calf, was asleep, his companion substituted it for his bull calf. Unknown to what had occurred, the rightful owner of the heifer calf congratulated his companion on his excellent fortunes and parted ways with his companion's newborn bull calf. Somali farmers/herders consider a heifer calf better than a bull calf since it will give milk and have calves when grown.

My next journey in life has led me to Dev Academy, where I hope to learn new skills and pursue a growth mindset.
I came from a small peninsula in the Horn of Africa, similar to the size of New Zealand, with approximately half the population of New Zealand. Somaliland was once part of the British empire as a protectorate when it gained independence on July the 1st, 1960.

Queen Elizabeth Alexandra Mary sent this letter to the people of somaliland on the day of their independence:"I, my Government and my people in the United Kingdom wish you well on this day of independence. The connection between our people goes back some 130 years and the British administration of the Protectorate for 60 years. I look forward to a continuing and enduring friendship between our two countries."
